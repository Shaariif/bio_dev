A Real Life Character

My name is Baba Saarif, and my life has been a journey of deep pain, searching, and personal transformation. In my teenage years, I was devastated by the tragic loss of my girlfriend, who died by suicide, leaving me broken and lost. This trauma led me into a dark period where I sought answers through religion. I immersed myself in the teachings of Islam, but over time I began to question everything and drifted into deism, doubting all organized faiths. Despite the confusion, I continued searching for truth, and after much reflection and study, I returned to Islam with a deep personal conviction, eventually writing a book based on my journey. However, when I tried to share my thoughts and the insights I gained, people mocked me, and I felt socially isolated. That loneliness led me to using weed as a way to escape, but it soon became a mental dependency that clouded my thoughts and trapped me. Afraid that I couldn’t live without it, I made the hard decision to become sober. Through discipline and clarity, I broke free from addiction and found meaning in my struggles. Now, I look at my past not as a story of failure, but as proof of resilience. I share this not for pity, but for those who are silently fighting battles like I did—so they know they’re not alone.

Life Lessons

  • Love at a young age can feel eternal — and losing it can feel like the end of everything. But it’s not. You are more than one heartbreak.
  • Religion or philosophy can guide you through emotional storms — but you must search sincerely, not blindly follow others.
  • People may not understand your pain. That doesn't make your story any less real or important.
  • Don’t let pain turn into addiction. What feels like escape at first can quickly become a cage.
  • You are not alone. Every broken heart, every doubt, every dark night — someone else has survived it too.
  • Your emotions are valid, but they don’t have to control your future. Let your experiences make you wiser, not weaker.
  • Love yoursel more then anything else.
